Food & Drinks

Boston Cream Pie


The dessert is a yellow butter cake layered with custard in between and topped with chocolate glaze. First created at one of city’s most iconic Omni Parker House hotel in 1881, which identifies more as a cake than pie. Boston cream pie is the official dessert of Massachusetts since 1996.

Clam Chowder


New England clam chowder (pronounced chowdah locally) is a rich and thick concoction of shellfish, bacon, potatoes, and cream. When you have it don’t forget the oyster crackers. New England Clam Chowder is the earliest-established and most popular variation of clam chowders in the nation. It is an iconic experience to get New England clam chowder in a bread bowl.

Lobster Roll


A sandwich native to New England, made of lobster meat served on a grilled top-split hot dog-style bun. The roll is usually served with drawn butter and lemon juice fillings, with some variations in the region served with mayonnaise. Whether the “right” filling should be melted butter or mayonnaise has sparked a huge debate.

Boston Baked Beans


They don’t call Boston “beantown” for nothing. The city got its nickname from the Boston Baked Beans, a variety of baked beans, sweetened with molasses, and flavored with salt pork or bacon. The dish is traditionally served in a small crock with brown bread on the side.

Samuel Adams Beer


Located right in Boston, The Samuel Adams Boston brewery offers public tours and tastings. Along with beer and merchandise sales at its two tap rooms and beer gardens in Boston. Some their most-iconic craft beers include the Samuel Adams Boston Lager, Sam ’76, and the New England IPA.

Cannoli


Cannoli is a fried pastry dough, filled with a sweet, creamy ricotta filling. While Cannoli is an Italian pastry, the rich Italian American cultural heritage in Boston’s historic North End neighborhood have made the pastry an icon of Boston. Mike’s Pastry, The three most popular shops for Cannoli in Boston are Mike's Pastry, Modern Pastry, and Bova's Bakery, all are located in the North End.

New England Clambake


The traditional New England clambake is a traditional method of cooking seafood and a summer classic. A New England clambake is usually made with steamed clams, lobster, mussels, potatoes, corn, onion and chorizo. Clambakes are also usually held on festive occasions in the New England region. While clambakes are often enjoyed at home or outdoors by the water, many seafood restaurants in Boston serves clambakes.

Roast Beef Sandwich


Roast beef sandwiches are one of Boston’s specialties. While Roast beef sandwiches encompass a large variety of sandwiches served with sliced roasted beef, Boston’s are traditionally topped with cheese, mayonnaise and barbecue sauce.
